Trigonal Bipyramidal Electron Geometries (AX5, AX4E, AX3E2 and AX2E3)
Molecules in this category may have three, four, five or six atoms. The configuration is specified as AX5 for the case with six total atoms, AX4E for the case with five atoms, AX3E2 for the case with four and AX2E3 for the case with three. Remember the "E" represents electron groups that are not involved in bonding, the "X" atoms around the central atom and the "A" is the central atom. Molecules of the type AX5 are described as having a trigonal bipyramidal molecular geometry, those of type AX4E  a seesaw molecular geometry, those of type AX3E2 a T-shaped molecular geometry, and those of type AX2E3 as linear.
